Problem solving

When I was blocked on a simple problem:

The first time that I was stuck on a simple problem was in the 'manipulating objects' kata. I know now that the reason was I simply didn't understand how objects worked. However at the time I was stumped. The problem solving techniques I used we first breaking it down, I tried breaking the problem into small steps, as in 'get up, walk out the door, do a barrel roll' small steps. However even working with that I could'nt solve the problem, so then I went to the next step, which was to use my google brain. I asked google 'JS how to access objects within objects' and then read through the results. This taught be the syntax and from there I was able to solve the problem. During that problem solving process I felt quite preassured. Knowing that I currently wasn't able to solve it made me think that I might never solve it, that put a feeling of preassure on me which ultimately slowed me down in solving it as it discouraged me. That did however leave me coming away from that having learned not to get discouraged, because despite my doubts I did solve the problem, and it didn't take that long after all (I think that problem specifically took me 30 minutes). Having learned that early on in the katas it helped me not to feel the same way later on in the module.

Elegantly solved a problem

A timer where I elegantly solved a problem was in the FizzBuzz and Super FizzBuzz stages. In the problem we had to take a list of number, and return the exact same list back, except for any numbers that are divisiable by: 15, 5, and 3. We would replace those numbers with "FizzBUzz", "Fizz", and "Buzz", before return them. The problem solving techniques I used for this stage were pseudocode. I wrote clear instructions for myself on how to form the conditional statements and functions. I then wrote out my code following those instructions and it worked first try. Then following on from this I worked on SuperFizzBuzz, in which I used a method called .map() (The .map function applies runs a function on each item of an array, or list. It is similar to a loop but much simpler to use in the situations where it can be used.) which I had researched previously and used it to apply the function I had just written to the lists that its tested with. becuase I had uesd clear pseudocode in the prevous step I had a very stable function that worked well, and it ran again with no issues. coming into this process I actually felt nervous. I saw alot of people struggling with the fizzbuzz problems, and so that made me nervous. It is also probably why I wrote such clear pseudocode. However once I got moving I felt alot more confident with it. And I felt proud of my solution at the end. The biggest thing I learned from this was the benefit of breaking a problem down into small pieces, as well as giving myself clear steps on how to solve the problem.
